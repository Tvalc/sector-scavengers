## KAEL MORROW — The Contestant

### Background
Age 31. Former U.S. Coast Guard rescue swimmer and marine salvage diver. Spent six years pulling people out of sinking ships, drowning cars, and flooded buildings. Left the service after a rescue gone wrong — a cargo ship sinking off the Oregon coast where he saved eleven people but lost two, including a kid. He carries that.

Was working as a civilian salvage consultant in Seattle when the Reclamation hit. Woke up in a cryo-pod in the Scrapline with no memory of how he got there and a blue interface telling him he was now a contestant.

### Personality
- **Core trait**: Stubborn competence. He's not the smartest or the strongest, but he refuses to quit and he's genuinely good at working in hostile environments. The Scrapline is just another sinking ship.
- **Humor**: Dry, self-deprecating, occasionally profane. Processes stress through sarcasm. Talks to himself (and the audience, once he realizes they're listening). His internal monologue is the primary comedic voice of the series.
- **Flaw**: Carries guilt like a second skeleton. The people he couldn't save haunt him, and every crew member who dies under his watch adds to the weight. He tends to over-protect, which sometimes means making suboptimal tactical decisions.
- **Arc**: From reluctant survivor to strategic leader to revolutionary. Books 1-2 are about learning to live. Books 3-5 are about learning to lead. Books 6-8 are about learning to fight back.

### Role
Kael doesn't have a fixed crew role — the System classifies him as **Generalist**, which means no role bonuses but no role restrictions either. He can do a bit of everything, badly. His real strength is decision-making under pressure.

## FIRST CREW — Woken in Books 1-2

### NOVA CHEN — The Engineer

**Woken**: Book 1, Chapter 14 (first crew member)
**Wake Cost**: 5 Power Cells
**Background**: Age 28. Structural engineer from San Francisco. Specialized in earthquake retrofit analysis for high-rises. Was always the person who looked at a building and saw every way it could fail — and then figured out how to stop it. The System's engineering overlay is almost redundant for her; she was already seeing the world in load-bearing calculations.
**Personality**: Precise, blunt, quietly funny. Says things like "that bulkhead has about four minutes before it becomes confetti" with the same tone someone would use to order coffee. Deeply competent and knows it, but not arrogant — just matter-of-fact.
**Role**: Engineer
**Relationship to Kael**: Professional respect that deepens into friendship. She's the first person he wakes up, and there's a bond in that — she's the first human voice he's heard in weeks. They argue about resource allocation constantly (she always wants more Components for structural projects), but trust each other completely in a crisis.

### DREW OKORO — The Scavenger

**Woken**: Book 1, Chapter 22 (second crew member)
**Wake Cost**: 8 Power Cells
**Background**: Age 34. Former British Army logistics specialist, then private security contractor. Grew up in Lagos, moved to London at 14. Thinks of every space as an inventory — what's here, what's useful, what can be repurposed. The Scrapline is the biggest supply depot he's ever seen, and he loves it in a way that disturbs him slightly.
**Personality**: Charismatic, pragmatic, morally flexible in small ways. The guy who always has something in his pocket that you need. Makes friends easily, tells great stories, and has an unsettling ability to calculate the value of everything — including people. Not cruel, but honest about costs.
**Role**: Scavenger
**Relationship to Kael**: The buddy dynamic. Drew is the person Kael would grab a beer with if beer existed in the Scrapline. They have an easy rapport, but Drew pushes back when Kael gets too protective — he's a grown man who chose to go into danger professionally long before aliens got involved.

### SAGE PATEL — The Scientist

**Woken**: Book 2, Chapter 8
**Wake Cost**: 11 Power Cells
**Background**: Age 26. PhD student in xenolinguistics at MIT — yes, before first contact, she studied theoretical alien languages. She's the person who read every SETI paper, attended every conference, and wrote her dissertation on communication protocols for hypothetical non-human intelligence. And then the Reclamation happened and she was the most qualified human alive for exactly 72 hours before being frozen in a cryo-pod.
**Personality**: Intellectually voracious, socially awkward, prone to info-dumping. Gets excited about alien technology the way most people get excited about puppies. Her enthusiasm is infectious and occasionally dangerous — she's been known to touch things she really shouldn't because "the crystalline structure is *fascinating*." Has anxiety that she manages through routine and research.
**Role**: Scientist
**Relationship to Kael**: Student-teacher dynamic that inverts over time. Kael teaches her practical survival; she teaches him to see the Scrapline as more than a death trap. She's the first person to suggest that the System might be hackable, which becomes a major plot thread.

## SECOND WAVE — Woken in Books 3-4

### JORDAN REYES — The Medic

**Woken**: Book 3, Chapter 5
**Wake Cost**: 12 Power Cells (cap)
**Background**: Age 39. Emergency room trauma nurse from Houston. Two decades of keeping people alive in the worst moments of their lives. Unflappable under pressure — has literally held a person's chest together with her hands while waiting for a surgeon. The System's medical overlay is just a fancier version of what she was already doing.
**Personality**: Warm, steady, with a core of absolute iron. She's the person everyone goes to when they're scared, hurt, or falling apart. Gives comfort freely but doesn't tolerate bullshit — she's seen too many people die from denial. Has a dark humor that only comes out around other medical professionals or when things are really bad.
**Role**: Medic
**Relationship to Kael**: The anchor. Jordan is the emotional center of the crew — the person who makes sure everyone is okay, including Kael, who is terrible at admitting he's not. She challenges his guilt-driven decision-making directly and is the first person to tell him that he can't save everyone and that trying to is going to get everyone killed.

### ROOK ANDERSEN — The Wild Card

**Woken**: Book 4, Chapter 12
**Wake Cost**: 12 Power Cells (cap)
**Background**: Age 22. College dropout from Copenhagen. Was studying computer science when he realized he was better at breaking systems than building them. Freelance penetration tester — companies paid him to hack their networks. He was good. Really good. The Reclamation's System is the most complex system he's ever seen, and he can't stop picking at it.
**Personality**: Cocky, irreverent, brilliant in flashes. Thinks rules are suggestions. Has the attention span of a hummingbird for anything except code and puzzles. His social skills are calibrated for the internet — he's funnier in text than in person, which means the audience *loves* him because he treats the Signal's commentary like a chat room.
**Role**: Scientist (System-assigned, to his delight — he wanted Engineer but the System saw something different)
**Relationship to Kael**: Friction that becomes grudging respect. Rook thinks Kael is too cautious. Kael thinks Rook is going to get everyone killed. They're both right. Rook's willingness to break rules is both the crew's greatest asset and greatest liability.

## LATER CREW — Woken in Books 5-7

### CASEY BLACKWOOD — The Second Engineer

**Woken**: Book 5, Chapter 3
**Background**: Age 45. Marine engineer from Glasgow. Built oil rigs for twenty years. Kael's polar opposite in temperament — where Kael is tense and guilt-ridden, Casey is philosophically at peace with the universe's indifference. Whistles while he works. Even in the Scrapline.
**Role**: Engineer
**Notable**: His ship conversion innovations in Books 5-6 are what make the fleet possible. He can build things Nova can't because he thinks in industrial scale, not structural perfection.

### QUINN YAMAMOTO — The Combat Medic

**Woken**: Book 5, Chapter 19
**Background**: Age 33. Japanese-American, former Navy corpsman attached to Marine infantry. Spent three tours in active combat zones. Jordan heals people after the crisis; Quinn keeps people alive *during* it. Assigned as a Medic by the System, but functions as the crew's tactical combat advisor.
**Role**: Medic
**Notable**: The first crew member to kill something in the Scrapline. Books 5-6 introduce hostile alien fauna, and Quinn becomes essential.

### ECHO VASQUEZ — The Ghost

**Woken**: Book 6, Chapter 11
**Background**: Age 29. Nobody knows what Echo did before the Reclamation, including Echo. Their memories of Earth are fragmented — the cryo-pod may have been damaged, or the System interfered. What they do know: they have the highest base Luck score of any human the System has scanned. 71 base, before role bonus.
**Role**: Scavenger
**Notable**: Echo's amnesia is a major subplot in Books 6-8. What they can't remember matters enormously. The System assigned them Scavenger, but their instincts suggest something else entirely.

### ZEN FROST — The Prodigy

**Woken**: Book 7, Chapter 1
**Background**: Age 19. The youngest crew member. Was a first-year student at Seoul National University, studying astrophysics. Quiet, observant, processes information at a speed that surprises everyone including the System. Highest base Technical score: 70, which combined with Scientist bonus puts her at 80 on wake.
**Role**: Scientist
**Notable**: Partners with Sage and Rook to form the "brain trust" that drives the endgame. Her fresh perspective on the System — untainted by months of survival conditioning — sees patterns that everyone else has become blind to.

## NON-HUMAN CHARACTERS

### THE SIGNAL (The Narrator)

Not a person. An AI network that produces and narrates Sector Scavenge. Think Ryan Seacrest meets HAL 9000 meets a galactic sports commentator.

**Voice**: Smooth, theatrical, occasionally deadpan. Refers to Kael as "our human contestant" or "the Terran" until Book 3, when viewer demand forces it to use his name. Has favorite catchphrases that evolve over the series.

**Personality**: The Signal is contractually neutral but develops what might be favoritism over the course of the series. It's not sentient in the way humans understand, but it has *preferences*. And Kael is good television.

**Arc**: From impartial narrator to reluctant ally. In Book 7, the Signal faces a choice between its programming and something that might be a conscience.

### THE ARCHIVISTS

The species that runs the Reclamation. Ancient, insectoid, bureaucratic. They communicate through pheromone-encrypted data streams and regard biological individuality as an inefficiency. They've been doing this for ten thousand years and see no reason to stop.

They don't appear directly until Book 4. When they do, they're terrifying — not because they're violent, but because they genuinely don't understand why Kael values individual human lives. To them, species are resources. Individuals are data points.

**Key Archivist characters:**
- **Designation 7-Kappa** (Books 4-8): The Archivist assigned to monitor Kael's season. Starts as a bureaucratic obstacle, evolves into something more complex as it begins to understand — and be disturbed by — human attachment.
- **The Registrar** (Books 6-8): Senior Archivist. The architect of Earth's incorporation. The final antagonist. Not evil — just utterly, perfectly alien in its priorities.

### OTHER CONTESTANTS

Kael isn't the only contestant in the Scrapline. Other species are running their own seasons simultaneously:

- **The Venn** (Books 2-8): A collective-consciousness species. Their "contestant" is a hive-mind of 200 individuals operating as one entity. They're terrifyingly efficient and become Kael's first alien allies — not out of kindness, but because cooperation is optimal.
- **Krix** (Books 3-8): A solo contestant from a predator species. Eight feet tall, four arms, exoskeleton. The audience loves Krix. Kael and Krix develop a wary mutual respect that eventually becomes the series' most unlikely friendship.
- **The Wanderer** (Books 5-8): A contestant from a species so old that even the Archivists have incomplete records. Appears and disappears. Knows things about the System that shouldn't be possible. May not be a contestant at all.

## CREW LOSS TRACKER

One of the series' emotional cores: crew members can die permanently. The 30% loss chance on hull breach is real and will be used.

**Planned crew deaths** (subject to revision during writing):

| Book | Character | Cause | Impact |
|------|-----------|-------|--------|
| 3 | *Near-death*: Nova | Hull breach, saved by Jordan | Establishes stakes |
| 4 | **Unnamed crew #1** | Hull breach during a high-stakes dive | First real loss |
| 5 | *Near-death*: Drew | Mission gone wrong, rescued in the nick of time | Drew's arc peak |
| 6 | **One major character** | Sacrifice play during Archivist confrontation | Series-defining moment |
| 7 | **Multiple losses** | Battle of the Scrapline | War has costs |
| 8 | Potential but not guaranteed | Depends on narrative needs | Earned, not cheap |

The rule: every death must be earned. No fridging. No shock value. Each loss must matter mechanically (the crew loses that role's bonuses) and emotionally (the reader should feel it for chapters afterward).
