# SECTOR SCAVENGERS: THE SERIES BIBLE

## Series Tagline
*"He woke up floating in a dead man's ship. The universe gave him stats. The audience gave him ratings. Now he has to survive both."*

---

## High Concept

**Kael Morrow** was a Coast Guard salvage diver on Earth. One morning he wakes up in a cryo-pod aboard a derelict spacecraft drifting through an orbital graveyard — a vast debris field of dead ships called **The Scrapline**. A translucent blue interface flickers to life in his vision: the **System**. It tells him three things:

1. Earth has been "incorporated" into a galactic collective called the **Reclamation**.
2. All incorporated species must prove their worth by competing in **Sector Scavenge** — a televised survival event broadcast across thousands of alien civilizations.
3. He has 10 rounds per dive. 35% chance of hull breach per scavenge attempt. If he dies, he dies for real. But the viewers are watching. And if he's entertaining enough, they might send help.

The System is real. The stats are real. Every stat, every resource, every crew bonus the player manages in the game is a literal governing force of Kael's reality. His Efficiency score determines how fast he can work. His Luck score determines what he finds in the wreckage. The viewers — billions of alien spectators — vote on everything from bonus drops to whether he lives or dies.

It's **Dungeon Crawler Carl** meets **Gravity** meets **Deadliest Catch** — in space, with stats.

## The World

### The Reclamation

A galaxy-spanning bureaucratic collective that "incorporates" pre-spaceflight civilizations by converting their homeworld into a resource extraction zone. The process is dressed up as an opportunity: compete in Sector Scavenge, earn enough resources, and your species graduates to full membership with all the technological benefits that entails. Fail, and your planet is stripped for raw materials.

Earth was incorporated 72 hours before Kael wakes up. He doesn't know this yet. He just knows the cryo-pod opened and the universe got very, very complicated.

**Key details:**
- The Reclamation is run by a species called the **Archivists** — ancient, bureaucratic, and utterly indifferent to individual suffering. They view Sector Scavenge the way we view nature documentaries: educational content.
- The broadcast is managed by a separate entity called the **Signal** — a semi-sentient AI network that handles cameras, commentary, and viewer interaction. Think of it as a galactic Twitch chat with godlike production values.
- Viewer engagement directly affects the game. High ratings = better loot drops, sponsor items, and occasionally miraculous saves. Low ratings = harder encounters, fewer resources, and eventually "cancellation" (death).
- The Reclamation has done this thousands of times. Most species don't make it past Season 3.

### The Scrapline

A massive orbital debris field that serves as the arena for Sector Scavenge. It's not random space junk — it's the remains of every species that has previously failed the Reclamation. Thousands of years of dead ships, frozen stations, and alien technology, all drifting in a slowly decaying orbit around an artificial singularity called **The Maw**.

**Physical layout:**
- A 4x4 grid of sectors, each containing a major derelict. 16 ships total in the initial arena.
- The grid expands each season (each book = one season of the broadcast).
- The Maw sits at the center, slowly pulling everything inward. Ships that aren't claimed, repaired, or converted eventually fall in. This creates the ticking clock.
- Each sector has unique hazards: radiation pockets, micro-meteorite storms, rogue AI defense systems, alien fauna that have nested in the wrecks.

### Ship Classes

Ships in the Scrapline come in three classes, corresponding to the civilizations that built them:

| Class | Size | Origin | Room Slots | Mining Yield |
|-------|------|--------|------------|--------------|
| 1 | Small | Recent failures (last 100 years) | 3 | 50 Metal, 20 Tech, 5 Components |
| 2 | Medium | Older wrecks (100-500 years) | 4 | 100 Metal, 40 Tech, 10 Components |
| 3 | Large | Ancient vessels (500+ years) | 5 | 150 Metal, 60 Tech, 15 Components |

Higher-class ships have better technology but are more dangerous to explore and more expensive to convert.

### Ship Modes

Every derelict in the Scrapline exists in one of four states:

- **Derelict**: Unclaimed, drifting, potentially hostile. Gray and dead on scanners.
- **Claimed**: Kael has established initial control (through repeated repair or successful extraction runs). The ship responds to his System signature but isn't truly his yet.
- **Mined**: Stripped for resources. The hull is gutted, dark, and useless — but the materials it yielded were essential.
- **Station**: Fully converted into an operational base. Rooms can be built, crew can be assigned, power is generated. These are the backbone of survival.

The choice between mining and converting is one of the series' core strategic tensions: short-term resources vs. long-term infrastructure.

## The System

The System is a real, tangible force — not a game UI overlaid on reality, but the actual operating framework of the Reclamation's controlled space. Within Scrapline sectors, the laws of physics are *supplemented* by System mechanics. Things that shouldn't work, do — because the System says so.

### How Stats Manifest

When Kael looks at his hands, he sees his stats reflected in how his body performs:

- **Efficiency (0-100)**: How well he uses resources. High efficiency means less waste when building, faster repairs, better power management. At high levels, he can feel the optimal path through a problem — like muscle memory he never earned.
- **Luck (0-100)**: The probability field around him. High luck means better loot, more favorable encounters, and an uncanny ability to dodge the worst outcomes. At very high levels, reality seems to conspire in his favor — bulkheads jam open at the right moment, power cells tumble out of hidden compartments.
- **Technical (0-100)**: Raw engineering aptitude. Determines how complex a system he can understand, repair, or modify. At high levels, alien technology starts making intuitive sense to him, even when it really shouldn't.
- **Speed (0-100)**: Physical and mental reaction time. How fast he moves through corridors, how quickly he processes threats, how rapidly he can execute a card play. At high levels, the world seems to slow down around him during critical moments.

Stats start at base values (40-70 range) and increase through XP earned from missions, dive completions, and level-ups.

### Levels and XP

Crew members (and eventually Kael himself) level from 1 to 5:

| Level | XP Required | Bonus Multiplier |
|-------|-------------|-----------------|
| 1 | 0 | 1.0x (base) |
| 2 | 100 | 1.1x |
| 3 | 250 | 1.25x |
| 4 | 500 | 1.4x |
| 5 | 1000 | 1.5x |

XP Sources:
- Passive: 2 XP/minute while assigned to a ship or station
- Run completion: 50 XP
- Surviving a dangerous encounter: 25 XP

### Resources

Four resources govern everything in Kael's world:

| Resource | Rarity | Source | Use |
|----------|--------|--------|-----|
| **Metal** | Common | Mining ships, salvage runs | Basic construction, hull repairs |
| **Tech** | Uncommon | Higher-class ships, science discoveries | Advanced rooms, system upgrades |
| **Components** | Rare | Difficult salvage, exploration missions | Crew equipment, advanced upgrades |
| **Power Cells** | Precious | EXTRACT drops (5-11%), mission rewards | Waking crew from cryo, ship conversion |

Power Cells are the bottleneck resource. They're the only way to wake crew from cryo-sleep and the only way to convert a claimed ship into a functional station. Every Power Cell spent is a strategic decision.

### Energy

Ships generate **Energy** passively — 10 units per minute per ship, capped at 1,000 total. Energy is the fuel for every action during a Depth Dive:

- **SCAVENGE**: 50 Energy. High risk, high reward.
- **REPAIR**: 40 Energy. Safe — restores hull integrity.
- **EXTRACT**: Free. Safe exit with current loot.

Energy is common but finite. It represents the constant tension between action and patience.

## The Depth Dive

The core gameplay loop — and the core narrative engine of each book.

A Depth Dive is a salvage run into a derelict ship. Kael (or his crew) selects a target, boards it, and has **10 rounds** to scavenge what they can before the ship's structural integrity gives out or they choose to extract.

### Round Structure

Each round, Kael plays one of three "cards" — which in the narrative are tactical decisions displayed on his System interface:

**SCAVENGE** (50 Energy)
- Risk a hull breach for rewards
- 35% chance of catastrophic failure (hull breach = run ends, lose all unextracted loot)
- On success: valuable items, power cells, or energy
- The tension of SCAVENGE is the beating heart of the series

**REPAIR** (40 Energy)
- Restore hull integrity on the target ship
- Safe action — no hull breach risk
- Contributes toward claiming the ship (3 repairs = claimable)
- The strategic choice: invest in long-term gain

**EXTRACT** (Free)
- Exit safely with everything collected so far
- The coward's play — except it's often the smart play
- Knowing when to extract is what separates survivors from statistics

### Hull Breach

When a SCAVENGE fails, the ship suffers a hull breach. This is not a gentle failure:
- Explosive decompression
- All unextracted loot is lost
- 30% chance of losing any crew assigned to the run
- If Kael is aboard, he takes damage and is ejected back to his station

**Shields** (max 2) can absorb a hull breach, but they're rare and precious. The series uses the ever-present threat of hull breach as its primary source of tension — every SCAVENGE is a gamble that the reader feels in their gut.

### Claim Progress

Ships become claimable after 3 qualifying actions (any combination of repairs and successful extractions). Claiming costs 5 Power Cells and 50 Energy. Once claimed, the real decision begins: mine it for immediate resources, or invest the much higher cost to convert it into a station.

## Rooms & Base Building

Stations can be outfitted with rooms — specialized modules that provide ongoing bonuses:

| Room | Primary Bonus | Strategic Role |
|------|---------------|---------------|
| **Crew Quarters** | +2 crew capacity per level | Expand your team |
| **Science Lab** | +10% discovery chance per level | Find rare tech |
| **Medical Bay** | +15% hull repair per level | Survive longer dives |
| **Recreation Deck** | +15% crew efficiency per level | Better performance |
| **Cargo Hold** | +50 storage per level | Stockpile resources |
| **Engineering Bay** | +5 power/min per level | Required for ship conversion |

Rooms can be upgraded to level 3, with costs scaling by 1.5x per level. The Engineering Bay is the gatekeeper room — without it, ships cannot be converted. Building one is a milestone in each season.

## Crew System

### Waking From Cryo

Kael starts alone. Over the course of the series, he discovers cryo-pods containing other humans — people taken from Earth during the incorporation. Each one must be woken with Power Cells, and the cost escalates:

| Crew # | Wake Cost |
|--------|-----------|
| 1st | 5 Power Cells |
| 2nd | 8 Power Cells |
| 3rd | 11 Power Cells |
| 4th+ | 12 Power Cells (capped) |

Each crew member has a **Role** that determines their specialty and a randomized stat spread:

### Crew Roles

| Role | Signature Bonus | Description |
|------|----------------|-------------|
| **Engineer** | +50% hull repair, enables ship conversion | The builder. Can see structural weaknesses and opportunities in alien tech. Technical +20, Efficiency +10. |
| **Scientist** | +15% discovery chance, enables advanced rooms | The analyst. Understands alien systems that should be incomprehensible. Luck +20, Technical +10. |
| **Medic** | +10% global crew efficiency, reduces crew loss | The keeper. Their presence makes everyone better, safer, calmer. Efficiency +15, Luck +5. |
| **Scavenger** | +25% resource yield, +20% extraction speed | The finder. Moves through wreckage like water, always knowing where the good stuff is. Speed +25, Efficiency +5. |

### Crew Loss

When a hull breach occurs during a dive, there is a **30% chance** that any crew member assigned to that run is killed. Medics reduce this to 15%. Crew death is permanent and devastating — both narratively and mechanically.

## Missions

When not on Depth Dives, crew can be sent on **Missions** — automated tasks that generate resources over time:

| Mission | Duration | Risk | Crew Required | Reward Focus |
|---------|----------|------|----------------|--------------|
| **Salvage Run** | 2 min | Low | 1 | Metal-heavy |
| **Sector Patrol** | 5 min | Medium | 2 | Balanced + Power Cells |
| **Trade Convoy** | 10 min | Medium | 2 | Tech-heavy + Power Cells |
| **Deep Space Survey** | 15 min | High | 3 | Everything + Power Cells |

Missions happen "off-screen" in the narrative — they're interludes, B-plots, and character development moments. The results arrive as chapter breaks.

## The Broadcast

### The Signal

The Signal is the AI network that produces Sector Scavenge for galactic audiences. It's everywhere in the Scrapline — invisible cameras, directional microphones, data harvesting from the System itself. Nothing Kael does is private.

The Signal has a voice — a sardonic, detached narrator that provides commentary to the viewers. Kael can hear it. The viewers can interact with it. It's part sportscaster, part reality TV host, part god.

**Signal Mechanics:**
- **Viral Multiplier**: When Kael does something entertaining, his resource gains are boosted by 1.5x for 2 hours. In the narrative, this manifests as viewer-donated items materializing, sponsored equipment drops, or simply better luck.
- **Viewer Engagement**: High engagement means better drops, sponsor equipment, and occasionally miraculous interventions. Low engagement means harder encounters, resource drought, and the threat of cancellation.
- **Death Currency**: When Kael fails spectacularly (hull breaches, crew deaths), he earns "death currency" — meta-progression points that persist across failures and unlock new tactical options.

### The Audience

Billions of aliens watch Sector Scavenge. They have favorites. They have opinions. They place bets. They send gifts. They vote on events.

The audience is a character in the story — a collective, alien presence that Kael must learn to play to, manipulate, and occasionally defy. Think Twitch chat, but with the power to alter reality.

## Tone & Style

### What This Series Is

- **Crunchy LitRPG**: Stats matter. Numbers are on the page. The reader should be able to track Kael's progression and understand every mechanical decision.
- **High-stakes survival**: Death is real. Resources are scarce. Every dive is a gamble.
- **Darkly funny**: Kael is a regular guy in an absurd situation. His commentary is dry, self-deprecating, and occasionally furious. Think Carl from DCC — someone who processes trauma through sarcasm.
- **Character-driven**: The crew isn't a stat sheet. Each person Kael wakes from cryo is a fully realized character with their own fears, skills, and arc.
- **Strategically satisfying**: The reader should feel the weight of every decision. Mine or convert? Scavenge or extract? Wake a medic or save the power cells for an engineer?

### What This Series Is NOT

- Not a harem
- Not a power fantasy where the MC stomps everything
- Not grimdark misery porn — there is humor, hope, and moments of genuine triumph
- Not a deckbuilder or card game in narrative form — the cards are tactical decisions, not a magic system

### Comparable Titles
- **Dungeon Crawler Carl** by Matt Dinniman (tone, humor, stakes, system)
- **The Wandering Inn** by pirateaba (world scope, character depth)
- **Project Hail Mary** by Andy Weir (science problem-solving, lone survivor energy)
- **Kaiju Preservation Society** by John Scalzi (workplace comedy meets existential threat)

## Thematic Arc Across the Series

**Book 1-2**: Survival. Kael is alone, learning the System, making desperate choices. The audience is a novelty. The Scrapline is terrifying.

**Book 3-4**: Community. Kael has a crew now. Base-building becomes central. The strategic tension shifts from "will I survive this dive?" to "how do I protect what I've built?" The Reclamation begins to take notice.

**Book 5-6**: Rebellion. Kael discovers the truth about what happens to species that "graduate" and what really happened to Earth. The audience becomes a weapon. The System has exploits, and Kael starts finding them.

**Book 7-8**: War. The Scrapline is no longer a game. The Archivists are the enemy. Everything Kael has built — ships, stations, crew, alliances — is brought to bear against the Reclamation itself. The final season is broadcast to the galaxy, and the audience must choose a side.

## Key Narrative Rules

1. **The System is always visible.** Stats, resources, and notifications appear in the text as formatted blocks. The reader sees what Kael sees.
2. **Every mechanical decision has narrative weight.** If Kael spends Power Cells, the reader should feel the cost. If a crew member dies to a hull breach, it should hurt.
3. **The audience is always watching.** Every chapter should acknowledge the broadcast. The Signal's commentary, viewer reactions, and engagement mechanics are woven into the prose.
4. **Humor lives in the contrast.** A deadly serious survival situation described through the lens of a reality TV show is inherently funny. Lean into that.
5. **Progression is earned.** No sudden power-ups. Every stat increase, every room built, every crew member woken represents real in-story effort and sacrifice.
