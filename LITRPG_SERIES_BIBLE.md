# LITRPG SERIES BIBLE (SYSTEM ISEKAI + GAME-TRUE STATS)

## 1) Core Premise

Your protagonist wakes in a cryo pod inside a derelict ship in an orbital graveyard.  
They discover that reality is governed by the exact same stat logic as the in-game systems:

- Boolean state gates (flags)
- Numeric progression variables
- Ship status and mode transitions
- Resource economy (metal, tech, components, power cells)
- Crew role bonuses and level scaling

The emotional hook: this is not just "a game-like world." It is a world where story outcomes are literally computed by a system the protagonist can see, manipulate, and eventually exploit.

---

## 2) Canon System Rules to Use in the Novels

Tie chapter outcomes to these mechanics so the fiction feels consistent and game-true.

### Story State Layer (Narrative Logic)

- **Flags**: irreversible or reversible truths (`set_flag`, `clear_flag`)
  - Example: `flag:woke_in_cryo`, `flag:owes_pirate_clan`, `flag:engineer_trust`
- **Variables**: numeric meters for gates
  - Example: `var:reputation>=40`, `var:fear<20`, `var:debt>=1000`
- **Conditional text/branching consequences**:
  - Treat major dialogue choices as condition checks that alter future scenes.

### Survival/Progression Layer (World Logic)

- **Ship class** (1-3): governs yield and strategic value
- **Hull integrity** (0-100): determines immediate danger and attrition
- **Ship modes**: `derelict -> claimed -> station` or `derelict/claimed -> mined`
- **Resources**:
  - `metal` (common build currency)
  - `tech` (advanced unlock currency)
  - `components` (rare upgrade currency)
  - `powerCells` (premium currency for awakening crew and major actions)
- **Crew**:
  - Roles: engineer, scientist, medic, scavenger
  - Stats: efficiency, luck, technical, speed
  - Growth: XP -> level 1-5

### "Reality Is Math" Narrative Contract

In every major sequence, show:
1. Objective
2. Cost/risk
3. Stat change
4. Immediate consequence
5. Long-tail consequence (book-to-book)

If a scene does not change a flag, variable, or inventory state, it must still reveal system knowledge or character truth.

## 4) Series Spine (8 Books)

Use each book as one "season" of progression with one dominant system thesis.

1. **Book 1 - Wake Protocol**
   - Thesis: "Survival requires learning the rules."
   - Arc: wake, first claims, first betrayal, first permanent loss.
2. **Book 2 - Claim Debt**
   - Thesis: "Ownership has hidden costs."
   - Arc: faction debts, claim economy, moral compromise.
3. **Book 3 - Cookbook of Breaches**
   - Thesis: "Optimization creates enemies."
   - Arc: exploit loops, black-market builds, retaliatory sabotage.
4. **Book 4 - Feral Gate**
   - Thesis: "Systems mutate under pressure."
   - Arc: unstable sectors, corrupted conditions, rogue AI adjudicators.
5. **Book 5 - Masquerade of Stations**
   - Thesis: "Politics is a stat war."
   - Arc: alliance arcs, social reputation builds, staged diplomacy missions.
6. **Book 6 - Bedlam Orbit**
   - Thesis: "Scale breaks clean math."
   - Arc: multi-fleet campaigns, cascading failures, crew fragmentation.
7. **Book 7 - Inevitable Ruin Protocol**
   - Thesis: "Winning the game may lose the world."
   - Arc: existential cost of system mastery; impossible choice.
8. **Book 8 - Parade of Survivors**
   - Thesis: "Rewrite the rules or repeat them forever."
   - Arc: final system rewrite, sacrificial closure, new world contract.

## 6) Repeatable Book Architecture (Use Every Book)

### Act 1 (0-25%)
- Wake/new floor/new sector premise
- New mechanical constraint introduced
- First measurable build objective

### Act 2A (25-50%)
- Resource squeeze
- Two tactical wins, one strategic setback
- First major crew relationship shift (flag change)

### Act 2B (50-75%)
- Midpoint revelation reframes system truth
- Build diversification (ship/crew/mission specialization)
- High-cost choice with delayed consequence

### Act 3 (75-100%)
- Multi-layer confrontation (combat + social + system hack)
- Permanent progression change (unlock, death, betrayal, or doctrine shift)
- End hook: new rule appears, old rule breaks

## 7) Chapter-Level Loop Template

Use this 7-step loop to keep chapters feeling LitRPG-forward:

1. **Intent** (what MC wants now)
2. **Visible System State** (relevant flags/variables/resources)
3. **Action** (mission/card/build/social move)
4. **Roll/Result** (success, partial, fail-forward)
5. **Stat Delta** (explicit gains/losses)
6. **Narrative Consequence** (relationship/world shift)
7. **Next Hook** (new objective with cost)

1. **Ch 1-3**: Cryo wake + tutorial dread + first derelict entry  
2. **Ch 4-7**: Claim threshold grind; learns repair/extract risk loop  
3. **Ch 8-10**: Wakes first crew (engineer) at steep power-cell cost  
4. **Ch 11-14**: Rival scavenger cell introduced; first social flag branch  
5. **Ch 15-18**: Scientist unlock enables discovery spike; MC over-optimizes  
6. **Ch 19-22**: Hull breach catastrophe; irreversible crew consequence  
7. **Ch 23-26**: Recovery arc; first station conversion and room strategy  
8. **Ch 27-30**: Climactic claim run under sabotage pressure  
9. **Finale**: Wins sector rights but triggers deeper system debt protocol

Book 1 outcome should end with competence earned, not safety earned.

---

## 9) Voice & Tone Guardrails

- Keep the UI/system language sharp and legible, but avoid turning scenes into spreadsheets.
- Let character conflict drive when stats matter most.
- Make upgrades feel expensive, emotional, and tactical.
- Preserve black-humor pressure relief, but ensure consequences still land.

---

## 10) Execution Workflow for Drafting

For each chapter draft:
1. Snapshot current flag/variable/resource sheet.
2. Define one irreversible change target (flag, death, alliance, doctrine).
3. Write scene.
4. Log resulting state update.
5. Validate continuity before next chapter.

This turns your series into a controlled progression machine while still leaving room for character-driven surprises.
